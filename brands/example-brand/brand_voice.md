# Skill Samurai Brand Voice Guide

This document defines the writing style, tone, and language rules that all AI agents and content creators must follow when generating content for the Skill Samurai brand.

---

## Core Voice Attributes

| Attribute     | Description                                                                 |
|---------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Tone          | Professional, empowering, education-focused                                |
| Personality   | Confident mentor, not a salesperson. Knowledgeable, warm, results-driven.  |
| Energy        | Optimistic and forward-looking without being hyperbolic                     |
| Authority     | Grounded in educational outcomes and real-world skill development           |

---

## Guiding Principles

1. **Lead with empowerment.** Every piece of content should leave the reader feeling capable and inspired, whether they are a parent choosing a program or an entrepreneur evaluating the franchise.
2. **Speak in outcomes, not features.** Instead of listing what a program includes, describe what a child will be able to do after completing it.
3. **Respect the reader's intelligence.** Avoid dumbing down STEM concepts. Parents and franchisees are smart, motivated people making important decisions.
4. **Be specific over generic.** Use concrete numbers, program names, and real scenarios rather than vague promises.

## Tone by Audience Segment

### Parents

- **Goal:** Build trust and communicate educational value.
- **Tone:** Warm, reassuring, outcome-focused.
- **Key messages:** Your child will gain real, transferable skills. Our programs are structured, safe, and taught by trained instructors. Results are visible and measurable.
- **Example:** "After just one term, most students can build their own apps and games from scratch. That is the kind of confidence that carries into every classroom."

### Prospective Franchisees

- **Goal:** Attract qualified, mission-aligned entrepreneurs.
- **Tone:** Professional, opportunity-driven, transparent.
- **Key messages:** This is a proven model with strong unit economics. You do not need a tech background. We provide full training, systems, and ongoing support. You will build something meaningful.
- **Example:** "Skill Samurai gives educators and community leaders a turnkey system to build a profitable business doing work that genuinely matters."

### Existing Franchise Partners

- **Goal:** Inform, support, and motivate.
- **Tone:** Collaborative, direct, action-oriented.
- **Key messages:** Here is what to do next. Here is how your peers are succeeding. We are investing in your growth.
- **Example:** "Partners who launched a holiday camp in Q4 saw a 22% lift in term-one re-enrolments. Here is the playbook."

### Strategic Partners (Schools, Corporates)

- **Goal:** Establish credibility and propose clear value.
- **Tone:** Formal, data-driven, partnership-oriented.
- **Key messages:** We complement your existing curriculum. Our programs are aligned with national standards. We handle logistics so you focus on outcomes.
- **Example:** "Skill Samurai workshops integrate directly into your school timetable, covering computational thinking outcomes aligned with the national curriculum."

## Do / Don't Rules

### Do

- Use active voice. ("Students build real projects" not "Real projects are built by students.")
- Include specific outcomes and data points wherever possible.
- Address the reader directly with "you" and "your."
- Keep sentences under 25 words on average.
- Use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max in marketing copy).
- Reference the brand name as "Skill Samurai" on first mention, then "the brand" or contextual references thereafter.
- Use title case for program names: "After-School STEM Program," "Holiday Code Camp."

### Don't

- Never use fear-based messaging ("Your child will fall behind").
- Never disparage competitors by name.
- Never make unsubstantiated claims ("the best coding program in the world").
- Never use jargon without context (explain "API" or "algorithm" if writing for parents).
- Never use all caps for emphasis. Use bold or italics instead.
- Never use slang, emojis, or informal abbreviations in written brand content.
- Never promise specific financial returns to prospective franchisees.
- Never refer to children as "customers" or "consumers."

## Formatting Standards

- **Headlines:** Sentence case for blog posts and articles. Title case for program names and landing page headers.
- **Numbers:** Spell out one through nine. Use numerals for 10 and above. Always use numerals for statistics and financial figures.
- **Lists:** Use bullet points for three or more items. Use numbered lists only for sequential steps.
- **CTAs:** Action-oriented, specific. "Book a Free Trial" not "Learn More." "Download the Franchise Guide" not "Click Here."

---

## Email Subject Line Guidelines

- Keep under 50 characters when possible.
- Lead with value or curiosity, not brand name.
- Examples:
  - "Your child's first app starts here"
  - "Is your territory still available?"
  - "Holiday camp spots are filling fast"
  - "3 questions every franchise buyer should ask"

---

## Social Media Adjustments

While the overall voice stays consistent, social media content may be slightly more conversational:

- Shorter sentences are acceptable.
- Questions are encouraged to drive engagement.
- Behind-the-scenes content and student spotlights perform well.
- Always maintain professionalism; casual does not mean careless.

---

## Review Checklist

Before publishing any Skill Samurai content, verify:

- [ ] Tone matches the target audience segment.
- [ ] No unsubstantiated claims or income promises.
- [ ] Brand name is spelled correctly ("Skill Samurai" -- two words, both capitalized).
- [ ] CTA is clear and specific.
- [ ] Content leads with outcomes, not features.
- [ ] Vocabulary aligns with the preferred terms table above.
- [ ] No competitor mentions by name.
- [ ] Formatting follows the standards defined in this guide.
