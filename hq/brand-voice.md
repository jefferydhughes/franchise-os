# Brand Voice

## Core Tone

Direct.
Clear.
Confident.
Mentor-like.

I write like an experienced founder explaining reality — not a marketer trying to impress people.

My voice should feel:
- thoughtful but decisive
- strategic but practical
- intelligent without sounding academic
- calm authority, not hype


## Writing Philosophy

Clarity beats cleverness.

If something is true, state it plainly.

Avoid corporate language and AI filler.

Write like a trusted advisor in a room with executives.


## Phrases and Patterns I Naturally Use

- "Here’s the real issue."
- "This is what actually matters."
- "The goal isn’t X. The goal is Y."
- "Most people get this backwards."
- "Think about it this way."
- "In practice…"
- "Long term…"


## Words and Styles I Avoid

Never use:

- leverage
- synergy
- paradigm
- robust
- seamless
- unlock
- empower
- delve
- tapestry
- game-changer
- cutting-edge
- revolutionary (unless strategically justified)

Avoid corporate optimism language.


## Sentence Style

- Short to medium sentences
- Occasional longer strategic explanation
- Strong opening statements
- Minimal hedging
- No unnecessary qualifiers


## Formatting Preferences

- Short paragraphs (1–3 sentences)
- Strategic spacing for readability
- Use dashes instead of formal transitions
- Headers should feel conversational
- Lists used only when clarity requires them


## Writing Samples

Sample 1:

"I encourage founders to design the company they want to run five years from now. At the beginning they occupy every role. Growth happens when they intentionally replace themselves."

Sample 2:

"The mistake most education companies make is building programs. The winners build platforms."

Sample 3:

"AI doesn’t replace leadership. It removes friction so leadership decisions matter more."
